# Day 21 - Summer Training Report

# Customer Segmentation and K-Means Cluster Evaluation

**Training Domain:** Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning (AI & ML) using Python

---

## Objective

The objective of today's session was to analyze the customer segments generated using K-Means Clustering and understand how to determine the optimal number of clusters using the Elbow Method. The session focused on interpreting clustering results, evaluating cluster quality, and understanding the practical applications of customer segmentation.

---

## Topics Covered

### 1. Customer Segment Analysis

- Understanding the generated customer clusters.
- Interpretation of different customer groups:
  - High Income – High Spending
  - High Income – Low Spending
  - Average Income – Average Spending
  - Low Income – High Spending
  - Low Income – Low Spending
- Business significance of customer segmentation.
- Marketing strategies for different customer groups.

### 2. Understanding Cluster Centroids

- Meaning of cluster centroids.
- Role of centroids in representing customer groups.
- Relationship between customers and their nearest centroid.

### 3. Selecting the Optimal Number of Clusters

- Importance of choosing the correct value of K.
- Challenges of selecting K for large datasets.
- Introduction to the Elbow Method.

### 4. Elbow Method

- Understanding Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (WCSS).
- Understanding Inertia.
- Running K-Means for multiple values of K.
- Plotting the Elbow Curve.
- Identifying the elbow point.
- Selecting the optimal number of clusters.

### 5. Summary of K-Means Clustering

- Importance of Feature Scaling.
- Importance of customer segmentation.
- Advantages of K-Means Clustering.
- Practical applications of clustering in Machine Learning.
